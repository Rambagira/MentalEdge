Why Your Brain Loves Breaks

The human brain isn't designed for marathon focus sessions. Research suggests that our attention spans naturally drift after about 20 to 30 minutes. When you try to push through this barrier, you experience "cognitive fatigue."

How It Works (The 4 Steps)

The beauty of this technique lies in its simplicity. You don't need expensive apps or tools; you just need a timer.

  • Step 1: Pick a single task. Multitasking is the enemy here.
  • Step 2: Set a timer for 25 minutes. Commit to working only on that task until the timer rings.
  • Step 3: Take a 5-minute break. Stand up, stretch, or grab water.
  • Step 4: After 4 "Pomodoros" (cycles), take a longer break of 15-30 minutes.

The Result?

By breaking work into manageable intervals, you create a sense of urgency that keeps you focused, while the frequent breaks keep your mind fresh. Try it today for just one hour.
