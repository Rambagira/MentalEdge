The Eisenhower Matrix: Master Prioritization

"What is important is seldom urgent, and what is urgent is seldom important." - Dwight D. Eisenhower. This quote is the foundation of the most effective prioritization tool in existence.

The Four Quadrants

Draw a square and divide it into four. Place every task on your list into one box:

  • 1. Do First: Urgent & Important (Crises, deadlines).
  • 2. Schedule: Not Urgent & Important (Exercise, planning, learning).
  • 3. Delegate: Urgent & Not Important (Some emails, scheduling).
  • 4. Delete: Not Urgent & Not Important (Social media scrolling).
Focus on Q2: Successful people spend most of their time in Quadrant 2 (Not Urgent, but Important). This is where growth happens and crises are prevented.
