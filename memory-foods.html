3 Foods That Boost Memory (Backed by Science)

Ever walk into a room and forget why you entered? Brain fog happens to the best of us. While puzzles and reading help, your brain is an energy-hungry organ that requires specific fuel to function.

1. Fatty Fish (The "Gray Matter" Builder)

Why it works: Your brain is about 60% fat, and half of that fat is the omega-3 kind. Fish like salmon, trout, and sardines are loaded with omega-3s.

The Benefit: Omega-3s are used by your body to build brain and nerve cells. They are essential for learning and memory.

2. Blueberries (The "Anti-Aging" Berry)

Why it works: Blueberries provide anthocyanins, a group of plant compounds with anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

The Benefit: Some of the antioxidants in blueberries have been found to accumulate in the brain and help improve communication between brain cells.

3. Pumpkin Seeds (The "Magnesium" Bomb)

Why it works: They contain powerful antioxidants that protect the body and brain from free radical damage. They are also an excellent source of Magnesium, Iron, Zinc, and Copper.

The Benefit: Low magnesium levels have been linked to many neurological diseases, including migraines, depression, and epilepsy.
